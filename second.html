    Diving into video games with the Wii and DS

    Like most people, my first consoles were from Nintendo. The Nintendo DS was my first video game console, and it introduced me to games that would help me formulate my preferences for games. Some of the more notable games were Star Wars Battlefront: Elite Squadrons, Mario Kart DS, New Super Mario Bros., & Lego Star Wars: The Complete Saga. My first memorable moments with video games come from some of these games. For example, I will never forget being stuck on World 8-8 for months when I was trying to beat New Super Mario Bros. for my sister. Soon after the DS came the Wii, which only allowed me to explore more titles, like Wii Sports & Wii Sports Resort, while also learning that video games could also be fun when playing with friends and family. Storytelling in games became more meaningful to me with titles such as Super Mario Galaxy 2, Star Wars: The Clone Wars - Republic Heroes, Rabbids Go Home, & Wall-E. These consoles showed me how different games could offer a fun experience, but, most importantly, they left me wanting a larger and more grandeur digital experience.

    Discovering games and their stories through Star Wars

    Although my first consoles were from Nintendo, the first time I played a video game on dedicated hardware was when I would play Star Wars: Battlefront II on my friend's PS2. This led to me eventually owning a PSP and playing the Galactic Conquest mode constantly and on my own. Not only did I become infutuated with games since then, but I also became an avid Star Wars fan. As I have become more invested in the video game industry, Star Wars has continued to be an essential factor that has refined which genres I enjoy. From Star Wars: Battlefront II (2005), Lego Star Wars: The Complete Saga, Lego Star Wars III: The Clone Wars, & Star Wars Jedi: Fallen Order, it is safe to say that Star Wars has had a massive influence on me will continue to have an impact into the future.

    The present & the future

    As of today, I currently enjoy unlocking new weekly cars in Forza Horizon 5, checking out Halo Infinite's multiplayer events, & getting some god rolls in preparation for Destiny 2: The Witch Queen during my free time. The upcoming Destiny expansion and seasons will keep me preoccupied as I wait for my most anticipated games which are Starfield and S.T.A.L.K.E.R 2: Heart of Chernoybl. I'm also interested in checking out Replaced due to its unique art style, and Redfall along with Deathloop since I am a fan of Arkane Studios' previous work. Lastly, I'm crossing my fingers in anticipation for a Cyberpunk 2077 next-gen update and expansion release this year. As always, I am excited to see what new experiences will be announced and will help grow both the video game and entertainment industry.

    Expanding through Xbox

    I was not able to regularly afford a new $60 game, so I started looking for a digital experience that was content heavy and would entertain me for a long time. In order to play with my friends and delve into sizeable content, I ended up acquiring an Xbox 360 with a Kinect by combining my Christmas and birthday gifts. From this point forward, I have primarily played on Xbox consoles and services. I mainly stuck to Microsoft's platform because all my friends played on Xbox, but I eventually decided to stay on with the Xbox Series X because of GamePass and Microsoft rewards. The Xbox ecosystem has played an important role in allowing me to explore different game genres without being restricted by money. GamePass has allowed me to discover new games and genres I didn't think I would be interested in. The Xbox team's significant progress towards their goal of providing a safe and user-friendly environment has also persuaded me to participate by joining their administrator program to help foster the Xbox community. I plan on sticking to the Xbox ecosystem, especially as the GamePass library increases due to recent acquisitions. I am curious to see how the Xbox community evolves and grows in the near future.

    Revolutionizing gameplay through deblob

    One of the more impactful games in my early gaming career was deBlob & deBlob 2. I first played deBlob on the Wii and was intrigued by the game's unique gameplay which revolved around mixing colors and 3D platforming. Therefore, when deBlob 2 released for the Xbox 360, I had to check it out to see how the first game's gameplay could be presented in a fresh manner. I was overjoyed when I experienced deBlob 2's revolutionized gameplay and storytelling. It showed me that games did not have to be limited to a specific formula of gameplay in accordance to the genre. Additionally, its catchy and impactful soundtrack solidified the importance of theming in video games. The simultaneous use of 3D platforming, 2D platforming, and third-person gameplay opened my mind to limitless reaches that digital experiences could reach.

    Gaming communities & Easter Eggs

    One of the first games I purchased for my Xbox 360 was Call of Duty: Black Ops II. The first-person shooter genre was not initially intrguing to me, especially the competitive multiplayer aspect, but my friends kept telling me about a zombie horde gamemode that I should checkout with them. This led to my dive into the Call of Duty: Zombies community. What kept me immersed in this gamemode and community were the elaborate easter eggs hidden on each map. Watching people figure out and decipher easter egg steps to ultimately unravel the lore and story of the Call of Duty: Zombies universe fasinated me. I enjoyed being apart of the easter egg hunt alongside my friends while also knowing that there was an entire community of people that shared that same enjoyment. Most importantly, I discovered how the relationship between the creators and players can cultivate a community that can enhance the experience provided by the game.

    Infinite Content in Minecraft

    Alongside Call of Duty: Black Ops II, I started playing Minecraft through the Xbox Live Arcade to hangout with my friends and to check out what the hype was all about. Unsurprisingly, I became heavily invested in Minecraft due to the gameplay offering a multitude of ways to have fun. Like Legos, Minecraft allowed my friends and I to use its existing sandbox to find ways to entertain ourselves. The occasional updates from Mojang only ensured that the game would continue to be entertaining well into the future. Minecraft ultimately provided me with a glimpse of how well made live service games could provide players with years of content and that video games did not have to be a digital experience that only last for limited amount of time.

    Thinking of A new meaningful experience

    Portal 2 provided me with one of my first memorable digital experiences that akin to a cinematic experience. The storytelling, soundtrack, and gameplay was unique and left a lasting impact on me which was rare, for me, at the time coming from a video game. I relished solving the game's unique puzzles and finding easter eggs on my own. The dedicated cooperative experience was one of the first that both my sister and I enjoyed. Portal 2 is also the first game that provided me with memorable characters such as GLaDOS, Wheatley, & Cave Johnson.

    Transforming the action adventure genre

    I intrgued by High Moon's Transformers games after being left wanting a more story rich Transformers installment after having watched the first three live action Transformer films. High Moon's use of the amazing visual style of the films mixed with the inclusion of a more meaningful story, resulted in one of my favorite games in the form of Transformers: Fall of Cybertron. Interestingly, this game led into my appreciation for competitive multiplayer games. The game rewarded teams for cooperation and teamwork which was different from my exposure to the run and gun style of Call of Duty. I began to realize that a competitive PvP gamemode could be fun if it provides players with more than one way to contribute to the team.

    Making your own fate on your own time

    When the Xbox One and PS4 generation of consoles was debuting, Destiny immediately convinced me to upgrade to a new console as soon as possible. The combination of the SciFi and MMORPG genre was attractive to me coming from my interest in Star Wars. From its release, I have regularly played Destiny and its corresponding sequels and expansions. Its deep and expansive lore along with the entertaining gameplay have kept me hooked. The Destiny franchise made its most meaningful impact on me through the cooperative raids. I cherish the memories I made from spending weeks trying to complete the Vault of Glass raid with my friends. Even more memories were made over the years as more raids and similar experiences have released. This franchise is the culmination of many aspects of gaming that I have always enjoyed and appreciated.

    Surviving solo & role playing

    I had been introduced to Bethesda's RPGs through Skyrim, but the medieval fantasy theme failed to captivate me completely. Naturally, I became aware of Fallout 3, which was dubbed as being like the Elder Scroll games except it was set in a post-apocalyptic world. This meant that when Fallout 4 was announced, I was significantly interested in finally getting involved in deep RPG game. Soon after, Fallout 4 became one of my favorite games and my most played single-player game. I was fascinated by the multi-layered storytelling and freedom given to the player. This Bethesda entry delivered to me the first single-player, role-playing experience that I truly became immersed into and fell in love with the world and lore. Fallout 4 showed me that the side quest and optional missions in a game could be just as meaningful and elaborate as the main quests. The love and care that can be found throughout Fallout 4 established to me that the people behind these games truly care about their creations.

    Violently shaking up the single-player fps

    During this time, first person shooter games had garnered the reputation as either mostly being looter shooters or competitive multiplayer games. Id Software's Doom 2016 reboot taught me that a modern FPS game could still provide innovative gameplay and a fun story. After enjoying my playthrough of Doom 2016, I was introduced to the world of Bethesda FPS titles like Wolfenstein, Dishonored, & Prey as I sought a similar similar experience. Since then, I have enjoyed the dynamic and unique gameplay offered by these titles. Their ability to merge existing gameplay genres to create their own unique iterations has displayed how games can continue to offer new and fresh adventures while telling a good story.

    The RPG of the future

    Having been a fan of the cyberpunk genre, Cyberpunk 2077 had been on my most anticapated game list for long time. Coming off my experience with Fallout 4 and its expansions, I relished in the ability to have a similar immersive experience in a cyberpunk world. Not only did Cyberpunk 2077 provide me with a similar experience, but it exceeded my expectations by putting forward a detailed and convincing world that had only previously been seen in AAA first-party games like The Last of Us and God of War. I was amazed by the acting and technology used to depict the characters and world of Cyberpunk 2077. This game served as my glimpse into the next generation of video games, and I can't wait to see what the future of RPGs will be now that a new standard has been set.
